### Project Management Processes

The core of the OctoAcme approach revolves around clearly defined roles and collaborative workflows. Project managers, engineers, QA specialists, and product owners each have documented responsibilities and structured interactions aimed at supporting transparent work, regular stand-ups, code and quality reviews, and milestone check-ins. Open communication is maintained through planned meetings, risk and issue escalation channels, and documentation updates. Post-release retrospectives encourage reflection and establish actionable improvements for future cycles.

Communication is a pillar of the OctoAcme method. Dedicated meetings, asynchronous documentation updates, risk reviews, and feedback loops keep stakeholders engaged and aligned across all stages. All decisions and rationale are tracked and stored, reducing project ambiguity and single-person knowledge dependency. This approach not only facilitates onboarding but also transforms tacit team insights into versioned, searchable artifacts.

Quality assurance is woven into every phase of the process. Formal peer reviews, explicit go/no-go checkpoints, and systematic testing are mandatory before code is released. Post-project retrospectives and continuous improvement practices ensure learnings are retained and improvements validated before being incorporated into future work.
